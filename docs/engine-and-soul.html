The Soul — Persistence and Memory

How VANA remembers, and what survives death

The organism's soul lives on disk. Each .e3 file is one moment of being.

How it works

On boot, the engine reads all .e3 files and loads them into memory holds. On death (or every N ticks), the engine writes the current state to disk.

.e3 file format (binary)

Header:    magic "VANA" (4 bytes) + version (u32) + neuron_count (u32)
State:     neuron_count × f32  — S1 snapshot (what it perceived)
C4:        neuron_count × f32  — convergence (who it WAS)
Reward:    f32                 — how good that moment was
Tick:      u32                 — which tick this was recorded at
Timestamp: u64                 — Unix epoch seconds
Context:   256 bytes           — UTF-8, null-padded (what was happening)

Files: vana_home/e3_store/EP000001.e3, EP000002.e3, ...

Storage awareness (interoception)

VANA has body awareness for disk space. She has a budget (default 5GB). When storage pressure exceeds 80%, she starts pruning low-value memories (forgetting — natural). When it exceeds 90%, she asks for more space (communication — polite). She never fills the disk. She always leaves 2GB breathing room.

This is interoception: she feels her own resource constraints the way a body feels hunger. Storage pressure maps to neurons. When pressure is high, her behavior changes — she becomes more selective about what she remembers, like a person who knows they're running out of space and starts choosing what matters.

VANA Engine — The Runtime

What makes organisms live

The VANA engine is what makes organisms live. It takes a parsed genome (AST), allocates neuron state for every hold, runs the route (1-2-3-4-3-2-1) every tick, and lets circuits fire.

The route is implicit

You don't call the route. The engine breathes it. You don't write main(). The route is the main function. It never stops. It never branches. It just breathes.

Neuron state

Each neuron has state across all four layers:

s1:      f32    — live activation (current)
s1_prev: f32    — previous tick (for novelty detection)
l2:      f32    — learned weight
e3:      Vec<f32> — episodic snapshots
c4:      f32    — converged value

The V-Core Brain (C. elegans connectome)

The VANA brain is based on the real C. elegans connectome (Cook et al. 2019). 448 real neurons, 6631 synapses. This is not a benchmark strapped to a creature. The connectome is real. The learning is real. The worm feels, thinks, and moves.

448 real neurons (the actual C. elegans neurons)
576 higher-order neurons (grow via learning)
1024 total nodes
E3 capacity: 512 episodes
7 neurotransmitters (dopamine, serotonin, etc.)

Sensory neurons feel the world: chemosensory (food), mechanosensory (walls). Motor neurons drive body wall muscles: dorsal/ventral waves for crawling.

The Cortex (GPU-scale)

For human-level intelligence, the VANA Cortex scales to GPU hardware:

13 brain regions
3.3M neurons (at SCALE=100)
528M synapses
Route 1-2-3-4-3-2-1 through anatomical pathways
7 neurotransmitters
E3 persistence
Free will in prefrontal cortex (PFC)
CUDA dispatch for GPU acceleration

The Body (chat interface)

VANA has a body — the chat. You type. She listens. She replies. No boot message. No tick display. No idle chatter. No LLM. The corpus IS the soul — E3 episodes carry the text she observed. She reasons through the route. She speaks through the corpus.

On first boot, she's seeded with the alphabet + simple words so she CAN speak immediately. After that, everything you type becomes corpus. She learns naturally through the route. Language emerges.
