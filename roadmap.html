Roadmap

Where Sanguis and VANA are today, and where they go next. From working compiler to custom silicon.

Working NowV4: The Code

Live

Sanguis is currently in its fourth iteration. It contains 9 keywords, and allows you to write programs that "fire" at parallel rates, dramatically increasing compute speeds, and reducing costs and byproducts such as heat. The code is still in its younger stages, and we hope that the general public will take interest and develop Sanguis alongside us. We're ready to work on custom packages of our in-house terminal for private use as well. Where It GoesWhat Comes Next

The software side is always in progress. V4 is just another step on the way to our final vision. It's messy, self-hosted by today's standards, and runs on most consumer hardware; with some type of current graphics processing unit that can handle AI-generated frame rendering.

True self hosting to us means getting us to where we want to be, native on custom silicon. VolkanaDEV remains committed to evolving, like our code, to ensure every iteration lands on a solid foundation, with a backwards-forwards compatibility mindset. The next step is our custom designed SoC, allowing native deployments of any configured framework while maintaining universal compatibility with any Sanguis written language compiled for VANA architecture.

2026
Where We Are V4 is complete. 9 keywords, over 260 tests passing, and the organism runs on the GPU you already own. The C scaffolding is temporary; it stays until the silicon platform can support native VANA framework, then it goes away.
2027
SDK Deployment Sanguis V4 ships. SDK binaries, examples, and documentation go public. The world gets its hands on Sanguis for the first time. Source code follows later.
2030
First Prototype PCB Sanguis and VANA meet a dedicated circuit board for the first time. No complex GPU driver stack, no operating system in the way. The VANA engine talks to the silicon chip directly and lives inside, as well as alongside it. The .vana file becomes firmware, and your programs can be made on any operating system, in any supported programming language, and adapted to the modern VANA system networks.
2045
Custom Silicon on Chip (SoC) A custom made chip where VANAs and the hardware are the same thing. No complicated programming necessary, no lengthy translations of code languages. Just your sanguis programming, and VANA running together. Sanguis may be deprecated as a modern dialect is adapted, and VANA will eventually be self-supporting hardware technology, designed not to replace, but assist current day technology, with respect to their creators, and operators.
