Roadmap

Where Sanguis and VANA are today, and where they go next. From working compiler to custom silicon.

Working NowV2: The Foundation

Live

Originally built as a re-design of the original V1 source code that powered Marrow, our in-house terminal user interface. Ended up working better than intended. Previous concepts were able to compile working applications, such as a small simulated connectome playing a video game, written in Sanguis. While not quite what we wanted, it's more than ready today, and will continue to recieve steady improvements and updates alongside it's 3.0 counterpart.

  • ✓.sang source compiles to .sirc, then to a standalone .exe. The whole pipeline works.
  • ✓139 tests passing. Text builtins work: read, write, extract, replace, concat, match, trim, crop.
  • ✓The .exe runs on any Windows machine. Zero dependencies. 25 MB installer.
  • ✓The GPU benchmarks on the Benchmarks page are real numbers from this engine, on an RTX 3060 and an i7-12700K.
  • ✓The Rust bootstrapper is scaffolding. It exists to get the language standing on its own. Once it does, the Rust goes away.

PrototypeV3: The New Language

Prototype

V3 is our semi-functional current project. A totally self-encapsulated, portable, and modular deployment of Sanguis and for use in future VANA systems. This version represents the most recent version of the new edition of Sanguis. Previous iterations of concepts based in langauges such as zig, python, and rust, resulted in roughly 20 working protoypes. Broken into purpose, about 14 of them were connectome or neural network style neuromorphic systems, and the rest were a conversion into the Sanguis language. While not quite ready, Sanguis V3 represents our future vision for using Sanguis across any desired configuration, or VANA deployment

  • ✓The compile chain works for simple programs. .sang to .vana genome, interpreter runs it. Hello world, multi-emit, greeter. Small stuff, but it runs.
  • ✓Native compilation works too. .sang to .vana to .exe. 5 out of 5 test cases pass. No interpreter needed on the target machine.
  • ✓The V3 compiler and interpreter are written in V2 Sanguis, not V3. That is the self-hosting path, but we are not there yet. The language is not building itself today.
  • ✓The .vana genome is pipe-delimited text right now because it is easy to debug. Binary comes later.

The Path ForwardWhat Comes Next

Moving forward to a complete Sanguis 3.0 platform will mark a major milestone in the VolkanaDEV ecosystem, and allow inital firmware deployments of custom hardware, such as custom design PCBs and small product prototypes. VolkanaDEV remains committed to evolving, like our code, to ensure every iteration lands on a solid foundation, with a backwards-forwards compatability mindset. The next step is our custom designed SoC, allowing native deployments of any configured framework while maintaining universal compatability with any Sanguis written langauge compiled for VANA arcitechture.

2026
Where We Are V2 compiles and runs. V3 is semi-functional. GPU benchmarks on real hardware. The Rust bootstrapper is still holding things up.
2028
Sanguis 3.0 Complete V3 writes itself. The Rust bootstrapper retires. Binary .vana genome. Apoptosis, where stalled bodies die on their own. The language runs on its own terms.
2030
First Prototype PCB A dedicated board. No GPU driver stack, no operating system in the way. The engine talks to silicon directly. The .vana file becomes firmware.
2045
SoC A chip where the language and the hardware are the same thing. No translation layers. No drivers. The code is the silicon.
What a future VANA could look like

Right now VANA runs on a GPU through Vulkan. It works, but it is still going through someone else's pipes. The PCB is the first time VANA gets its own hardware. No driver stack. No operating system abstraction. Just the engine and the silicon. Same language. Same .vana file. Different world underneath. That is the 2030 goal, and everything between here and there is the work to get there.
