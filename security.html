Safety & Licenses

Progress belongs to all of us, and at VolkanaDEV, we built gentle, yet firm rules to make sure it is used to help, never to hurt. These rules are not optional, but they won't get in your way.

VolkanaDEV remains committed to a healthy relationship with current technology, companies, or individuals, and ensures a smooth and careful transition to any future software or hardware.

The Shared Code Promise

You can read, test, and adapt our code freely. If you modify the engine and run it for users over a network, you must release your changes publicly. No secret monopolies.

Strict Anti-Harm Rules

Our license forbids this framework from being used for autonomous weapons, mass surveillance, facial recognition without consent, or tools designed to exploit people.

Immediate Toxic Breach

Break the anti-harm rules and your license terminates instantly. You assume all legal liability, lose all security updates, and are permanently locked out of VolkanaDEV networks.

Deploying anything publicly? If you want to deploy, distribute, or make available any Sanguis or VANA based software, hardware, network, or any other VolkanaDEV technology for commercial or public use, or as a limited-scope large-entity deployment, you need a contract with us first. The VPL-1.0 lets you read, test, and adapt the code freely, but commercial or public deployment requires a separate deployment agreement with VolkanaDEV. Contact volkanadev@volkanadev.com or visit our contact page to get started.

The FrameworkHow the Rules Work

The ability to create with Sanguis belongs in the hands of the individual creator. But something this advanced needs careful supervision.

That is why we created the VPL-1.0. It keeps our software open and inspectable for independent developers, students, and researchers, while installing a legal wall against weaponization or exploitation.

For government agencies and large entities, we offer a separate contract under what is known as a "Cleave Clause": the VolkanaDEV Government Infrastructure Contract. It grants a private, perpetual, non-transferable, non-sublicensable license for a specific approved scope. It overrides Section 2 of the VPL-1.0 on a per-project basis for defense systems. It requires formal contract execution carefully negotiated with VolkanaDEV. See the Contact page to request one.

The Government Contract includes four binding provisions: a carve-out that supersedes the public or commercial restrictions in the context of autonomous defense systems, a packaged handoff that gives the Licensee absolute custody with zero Licensor data access, a maintenance clause requiring cryptographically signed security updates with no operational interference, and an indemnification clause delivering the engine "as is" with full Licensee liability. These are summarized in Section 5 of the license below.
